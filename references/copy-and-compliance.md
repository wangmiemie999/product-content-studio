# Copy and Compliance Rules

## Evidence Levels

- State confirmed specifications directly.
- Phrase supported benefits conservatively.
- Keep inferences out of customer-facing copy unless the user confirms them.
- Present conflicts and missing facts in a review section, not as marketing claims.

## Prohibited Invention

Never invent efficacy, safety, medical outcomes, ingredient properties, environmental benefits, compatibility, durability, origin, certification, test results, exclusivity, scarcity, rankings, endorsements, or competitor comparisons.

## Sensitive Categories

For food, supplements, cosmetics, medical products, children's products, electrical goods, protective equipment, and other regulated categories:

1. Identify the target market and channel if material claims are requested.
2. Preserve required warnings and limitations from the source.
3. Avoid diagnosis, treatment, prevention, guaranteed outcomes, or unverified safety claims.
4. Flag claims that require legal, regulatory, or platform review.
5. Do not infer compliance from symbols that are unreadable or ambiguous.

## Style

- Prefer specific nouns and measurable facts over superlatives.
- Keep titles readable and avoid keyword stuffing.
- Turn features into benefits only when the connection is reasonable and non-guaranteed.
- Do not describe props shown in lifestyle images as included items.
